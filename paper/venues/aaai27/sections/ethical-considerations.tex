\section*{Ethical Considerations}

The work studies conversion of synthetic 3D assets for AI data pipelines rather
than human-subject data. The main ethical risks are indirect: synthetic scenes
can still encode dataset bias, licensing constraints, unsafe deployment
assumptions, or misleading claims about downstream model robustness. Any
submission using converted synthetic scenes should document asset provenance,
license compatibility, filtering criteria, and the intended use of generated
renders. If converted scenes are used
to evaluate or train vision-language models, the paper should avoid overstating
real-world generalization and should report failure cases where material
simplification changes object affordances, safety cues, texturing, or semantic
grounding signals.

We also used AI coding, writing, and image-generation assistants for
implementation support, manuscript editing, audit drafting, and the schematic
method-chain roadmap in Figure~\ref{fig:acl_method_chain}. The generated
schematic is not experimental evidence; all quantitative claims, citations,
reported artifacts, and empirical visual panels were checked against repository
evidence and cited sources by the authors.
