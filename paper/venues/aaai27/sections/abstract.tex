\begin{abstract}
Synthetic 3D benchmarks increasingly evaluate vision-language and embodied
agents through rendered USD scenes, yet the material conversions needed for
portability are usually treated as neutral infrastructure. We study
MDL-to-UsdPreviewSurface conversion as a controlled within-simulation
perturbation for language grounding and embodied-data reliability. A
composition-preserving conversion path replaces MDL shader networks with
PreviewSurface approximations while keeping matched original/converted USD
evidence. On four Isaac Sim assets, proxy similarity remains high (PSNR
35.52\,dB, SSIM 0.990, LPIPS 0.020, CLIP 0.925, DINOv2 0.872), but proxy
fidelity alone is insufficient for downstream grounding claims. We therefore
evaluate GRScenes with a 15-pair clean visual-QA pilot and a frozen 30-pair
target-centered material-shift stress set. Gemma4 preserves 30/30
target-category answers in both conditions and scores 27/30 original versus
29/30 converted normalized-1000 point hits; Qwen2.5-VL exposes a
raw-coordinate preference under the same prompt contract. We further add a
selected NVIDIA-baseline
material-effect audit and a 99-episode official InternNav sanity run. The
result is a claim-bounded evaluation
protocol: visual similarity, grounding, material-effect risk, and embodied-stack
usability should be reported as separate evidence gates before converted
synthetic scenes support downstream robustness claims.
\end{abstract}
