\section*{Ethical Considerations}

The work studies conversion of synthetic 3D assets for AI data pipelines rather
than human-subject data. The main ethical risks are indirect: synthetic scenes
can still encode dataset bias, licensing constraints, unsafe deployment
assumptions, or misleading claims about downstream model robustness. Any ACL
submission should document asset provenance, license compatibility, filtering
criteria, and the intended use of generated renders. If converted scenes are used
to evaluate or train vision-language models, the paper should avoid overstating
real-world generalization and should report failure cases where material
simplification changes object affordances, safety cues, texturing, or semantic
grounding signals.
