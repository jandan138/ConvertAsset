\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Protocol diagnostics.}
This paper contributes a measurement protocol: noMDL is a visible intervention,
and the study reports measured effects rather than safety, speed, or quality
ranking between ConvertAsset and NVIDIA's route. Rendered scenes are data; the
route gives context for the tested path while tool ranking remains outside this study. The
evaluation is component-wise: proxy similarity, VLM answers, point grounding,
coordinate checks, material-risk bins, and stack-entry checks stay separate. One
check can pass while a probe still sees a change.

\paragraph{Scoring format before deltas.}
The GRScenes stress result shows why proxy similarity is not enough: a close
image can still expose a prompt-and-coordinate mismatch. Gemma4
follows the normalized-1000 format, while \mbox{Qwen2.5-VL} shows a raw-pixel
preference under the same prompt family. This backend difference is a protocol
diagnostic.
Coordinate frames and parser coverage come first; only then can a conversion
delta become evidence.

\paragraph{Keep the tested settings visible.}
This audit keeps static conversion, zero-MDL output, and effect-token
coverage separate from broad success statements. The four GRScenes bins support
visual checks; clearcoat and texture risks remain separate limitation cases. The
99-episode KuJiaLe run
checks InternNav on three official scenes; it gives entry evidence for that
setting. Portable scenes should therefore be reported with the data
and setting that were actually tested.
