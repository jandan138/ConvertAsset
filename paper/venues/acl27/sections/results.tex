\section{Results}

\subsection{Proxy Similarity Is Necessary but Not Sufficient}
\label{sec:proxy_results}

The first result is scoped to proxy preservation. Across four single-object Isaac Sim
assets, converted and source MDL views stay close by proxy metrics. Mean PSNR
is 35.52\,dB, SSIM 0.990, LPIPS 0.020, CLIP 0.925, and DINOv2 0.872 over 24
matched pairs. Those numbers pass this visual check while leaving grounding
behavior unresolved. A close render can still alter the target, point frame, or
material cue. We use proxy match as a prerequisite for later task checks. The
render panel gives context; reported numbers come from frozen
tables.

\subsection{VLM Grounding Under a Frozen Material Perturbation}
\label{sec:vlm_grounding_pilot}

The clean GRScenes pool has 15 source/noMDL pairs that pass QA. It serves as a
pilot preservation control because it remains below the configured 20-pair clean
final set. The lower row of
Figure~\ref{fig:render_scene_evidence_acl} anchors the section in actual
commercial-interior renders; reported numbers remain tied to frozen pair tables.

\input{tables/tab_grscenes_vlm_clean_pool_pass15}

Table~\ref{tab:grscenes_vlm_clean_pool_pass15} separates answer stability from
point-grounding stability. Gemma4 answers all 30 clean-pool renders correctly,
with normalized-1000 point hits of 8/15 original and 6/15 converted. Qwen2.5-VL
has lower parser coverage: 23/30 scorable answer rows, raw point hits of
5/14 and 5/15, and zero normalized-1000 point hits in both conditions.

The expanded stress set moves to the task question: when the target stays
visible but the material channel can move, does the same prompt-and-coordinate format still
bind model output to the same object? We freeze 30 target-centered PASS/WARN
pairs for this test.

\input{tables/tab_grscenes_vlm_stress_expanded30}

Table~\ref{tab:grscenes_vlm_stress_expanded30} gives the main result for this
frozen stress test. Gemma4 keeps all 30 target labels in both material
conditions. Under the normalized-1000 scoring format, it scores 27/30 point hits on
original renders and 29/30 on converted renders; 28 pairs keep the same hit
status. Qwen2.5-VL has another coordinate preference: raw-pixel hits are 22/29
original and 19/29 converted, while normalized-1000 hits stay 3/29 for both
versions. The comparison should be read as a protocol diagnostic: material
shift, prompt schema, and coordinate frame meet at the measurement interface.

\input{tables/tab_reviewer_closure_paired_ci}

The paired bootstrap intervals in Table~\ref{tab:reviewer_closure_paired_ci}
quantify that comparison. On this 30-pair stress set, Gemma4 shows a small
point-hit gain, while Qwen's raw pixel diagnostic drops on converted renders.
The intervals describe frozen evidence pools.

The VLM-grounding result comes from the recorded answer and point tables.
Figures show the visible shift and provide context for those measurements.
Overlays are only guides until the exact renders have clean logs and traces.

\subsection{Stack-Entry Evidence and Official Scene Stability}
\label{sec:embodied_sanity}

The final check asks whether converted scenes can still enter an executable
embodied stack. The official
KuJiaLe \texttt{val\_unseen} route covers 99 paired InternNav/DualVLN episodes
across three local scenes. Means are SR 0.5253/0.4848, SPL 0.4739/0.4298, NE
3.6798/3.6306, and TL 6.9754/7.0598.
The paired confidence intervals in Table~\ref{tab:reviewer_closure_paired_ci}
cross zero for SR, SPL, NE, and TL. This yields a limited result: the selected
official stack can load, render, and evaluate converted scenes end-to-end.
Rollout stills and videos are qualitative checks tied to the 99-episode paired
run.

\input{tables/tab_official_scene_performance_summary}

Table~\ref{tab:official_scene_performance_summary} reports load/render stability
for the same three official scenes. The 18 required paired runs all
finish in fresh launches for both asset families (18/18). Warm-up ready time is
similar for the two families. This check covers load/run stability only; speed
is outside the reported measurement. We omit the NVIDIA row until its converted scenes pass this load/render
check.
