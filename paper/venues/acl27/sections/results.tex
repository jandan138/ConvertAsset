\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Proxy Similarity Is Necessary but Not Sufficient}
\label{sec:proxy_results}

On the four single-object Isaac Sim assets, the converted renders remain close
to the original MDL renders under standard proxy metrics: mean PSNR is
35.52\,dB, mean SSIM is 0.990, mean LPIPS is 0.020, CLIP cosine similarity is
0.925, and DINOv2 cosine similarity is 0.872 over 24 matched render pairs.
These measurements justify using the converted assets for deeper checks, but
they do not by themselves prove language-grounding preservation. The rest of
the paper therefore treats proxy similarity as a screening gate, not as the
main ACL claim.

\subsection{VLM Grounding Under a Frozen Material Perturbation}
\label{sec:vlm_grounding_pilot}

The clean GRScenes pool contains 15 visual-QA PASS original/noMDL pairs. It is
a pilot preservation control because it remains below the configured 20-pair
clean final gate.

\input{tables/tab_grscenes_vlm_clean_pool_pass15}

Table~\ref{tab:grscenes_vlm_clean_pool_pass15} shows that answer-level
recognition can remain stable while point grounding is less stable. Gemma4
answers all 15 original and 15 converted renders correctly, but normalized-1000
point hits are 8/15 for original and 6/15 for converted. Qwen2.5-VL has lower
parser and coordinate stability: its structured-text run produces 23/30
scorable answer rows, raw point hits of 5/14 and 5/15, and zero normalized-1000
point hits in both material conditions.

The expanded stress set asks a different question. It freezes 30
target-centered PASS/WARN pairs where the target remains visible but material,
color, or lighting changes may be apparent.

\input{tables/tab_grscenes_vlm_stress_expanded30}

Table~\ref{tab:grscenes_vlm_stress_expanded30} reports the ACL-facing stress
result. Gemma4 preserves 30/30 target-category answers for both original and
converted renders. Under the primary normalized-1000 coordinate contract, it
scores 27/30 point hits on original renders and 29/30 on converted renders,
with 28/30 pairwise hit-status agreement. Qwen2.5-VL is different: it is more
comfortable in raw pixel space, with raw point hits 22/29 original and 19/29
converted, while normalized-1000 hits remain 3/29 for both conditions. This is
the main protocol finding: material perturbation, prompt contract, and
coordinate interpretation interact, so VLM grounding reliability cannot be
inferred from visual similarity alone.

\input{tables/tab_reviewer_closure_paired_ci}

The paired bootstrap intervals in Table~\ref{tab:reviewer_closure_paired_ci}
calibrate the same point. On the expanded30 stress set, Gemma4 has a small
positive converted-minus-original normalized-point delta, while the Qwen raw
point diagnostic decreases on converted renders. These are descriptive
intervals over frozen evidence pools, not population-level guarantees.

\subsection{Material Effects and NVIDIA Baseline Boundaries}
\label{sec:material_effect_baseline}

We next audit whether material mechanisms explain conversion risk and whether
the NVIDIA Asset Converter baseline changes the interpretation.

\input{tables/tab_material_effect_risk_matrix}

Table~\ref{tab:material_effect_risk_matrix} is the claim-boundary artifact. For
opacity/transparency, emission, normal/bump, and displacement/height, the
selected GRScenes pool has bounded static and selected qualitative evidence for
original MDL, ConvertAsset noMDL, and the NVIDIA PreviewSurface baseline. This
supports a covered-bin comparison, not a universal visual-quality win. The
supplemental clearcoat case is a selected NVIDIA target-loss failure while
ConvertAsset retains an approximated PreviewSurface target. Procedural texture
is a limitation case for both converters because checker/base-color texture
preservation evidence is lost after conversion.

\subsection{Embodied-Data Sanity and Official Scene Stability}
\label{sec:embodied_sanity}

For downstream embodied-data sanity, the official KuJiaLe
\texttt{val\_unseen} route covers 99 paired InternNav/DualVLN episodes across
three local official scenes. Combined original/noMDL means are SR
0.5253/0.4848, SPL 0.4739/0.4298, NE 3.6798/3.6306, and TL 6.9754/7.0598. The
paired confidence intervals in Table~\ref{tab:reviewer_closure_paired_ci}
cross zero for SR, SPL, NE, and TL, so the supported conclusion is scoped:
converted scenes can be evaluated end-to-end in a real official navigation
stack, but the current evidence does not establish broad embodied-navigation
robustness.

\input{tables/tab_official_scene_performance_summary}

Table~\ref{tab:official_scene_performance_summary} adds the official-scene
load/render stability check. All 18 required original/noMDL fresh-process runs
complete successfully across the same three official scenes. Aggregated
warmed-ready time is comparable between original MDL and noMDL, with
overlapping confidence intervals. We therefore use this evidence for
loadability and stability only. We do not report an NVIDIA official-scene
performance row because matching official-scene NVIDIA conversions have not yet
been generated and smoke-gated.
