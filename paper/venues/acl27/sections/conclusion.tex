\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper reframes synthetic-scene material conversion as a benchmark
reliability problem for vision-language grounding. On the four single-object
Isaac Sim assets retained from the workshop study, MDL-to-UsdPreviewSurface
conversion preserves high paired visual fidelity and strong CLIP/DINOv2 feature
similarity. On the GRScenes route, however, the pilot VLM probes show why those
metrics are not enough for language-grounding claims: answer recognition, point
grounding, coordinate interpretation, and visible material shifts can separate
from pixel-level similarity.

The practical outcome is a claim discipline, plus one frozen material-shift
stress set. Clean visual-QA pools and material-shift stress pools should be
reported separately. Coordinate contracts should be frozen before model
scoring. Raw-image coordinate rescoring should remain diagnostic when the prompt
asks for normalized coordinates. Failure cases should be registered as
qualitative evidence unless their sampling rule supports distributional
statistics. Under that discipline, material conversion becomes useful not only
for portability, but also as a controlled stressor for testing whether VLM
grounding evaluations are robust to rendering-pipeline changes.

The current ACL version is therefore a stress-protocol paper rather than a
completed embodied benchmark paper. The next step is to close the clean
preservation branch and, if the claim is extended beyond image-level grounding,
add navigation or manipulation results through an explicit embodied framework
rather than inferring downstream robustness from VLM image probes alone.
