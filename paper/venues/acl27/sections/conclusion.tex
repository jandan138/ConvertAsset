\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

USD material conversion changes the evidence that a
vision-language system sees, so it should be reported as a grounding
intervention rather than absorbed into preprocessing.

We evaluate that intervention with four checks: render match, grounding QA,
material-risk bins, and stack entry. Together they show where the recorded
settings are stable and where the converted asset should not be treated as a
benchmark substitute.
