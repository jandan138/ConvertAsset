\section*{Limitations}

The current evidence base is intentionally narrow: the workshop-era experiments
cover a small asset set, limited scene diversity, and mostly visual or
feature-level metrics. These results support claims about material-conversion
fidelity and rendering behavior, but they do not yet establish broad downstream
benefits for language, vision-language, or embodied-agent models. The ACL version
therefore requires additional experiments that connect converted synthetic
scenes to multimodal grounding tasks, captioning or question-answering behavior,
scene-text alignment, or agent instruction-following robustness. The current
pipeline also depends on Isaac Sim and USD assets whose licenses, material
graphs, and renderer settings may differ across datasets, so any general
guidelines should be validated on a larger and more heterogeneous scene corpus.
