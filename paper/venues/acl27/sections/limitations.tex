\section*{Limitations}

The current evidence base is intentionally narrow: the workshop-era experiments
cover a small asset set, limited scene diversity, and mostly visual or
feature-level metrics. The GRScenes extension adds real VLM grounding probes,
but these probes are still pilot diagnostics rather than final benchmark
evidence: the clean visual-QA pool has 15 pairs, below the configured 20-pair
final gate, and the zoom pool is a material-shift stress set rather than a
clean preservation set. These results support claims about measurement
protocols and model sensitivity, but they do not yet establish broad downstream
benefits for language, vision-language, or embodied-agent models. The ACL
version therefore still requires a larger canonical VLM run, clearer coordinate
semantics for all model backends, and any navigation or manipulation extension
needed for embodied-agent claims. The current pipeline also depends on Isaac
Sim and USD assets whose licenses, material graphs, and renderer settings may
differ across datasets, so any general guidelines should be validated on a
larger and more heterogeneous scene corpus.
