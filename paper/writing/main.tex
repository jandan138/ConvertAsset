\begin{abstract}
Physics-based simulation platforms such as NVIDIA Isaac Sim use MDL (Material
Definition Language) materials to achieve photorealistic rendering, but these
materials limit interoperability with standard 3D tools and web-based viewers.
We present a systematic multi-level evaluation of MDL-to-UsdPreviewSurface material
simplification, measuring its impact across four dimensions: pixel-level image
quality (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS), rendering performance (load time, GPU memory, FPS),
feature-level semantic preservation (CLIP and DINOv2 cosine similarity), and
downstream AI task performance (zero-shot YOLOv8 detection confidence). Our
experiments on four chest-of-drawers assets rendered from six viewpoints show
that the conversion preserves high visual fidelity (mean PSNR 35.52\,dB, SSIM
0.990, LPIPS 0.020), maintains strong semantic similarity (CLIP cosine
similarity 0.925), and is detection-neutral in zero-shot object detection,
with negligible impact on rendering performance. We release ConvertAsset, an
open-source toolkit automating the conversion pipeline with
composition-preserving USD traversal, and provide practical guidelines for
synthetic data practitioners on when material simplification is safe for AI
training pipelines.
\end{abstract}
